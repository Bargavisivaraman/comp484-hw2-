Conveying meaning through structure

Creating properly structured pages is the foundation of any good website. A solid semantic structure allows you to convey meaning and properly represent content types through nothing more than the page's markup. Without a consistent, semantic structure your site will be less effective.

Sectioning elements

One way to add meaning to your content is to organize it into sections that convey meaning and structure.

In previous versions of HTML, content was grouped using heading tags and divs. HTML5 introduced semantic elements like article, section, nav, header, footer, and aside to improve clarity and accessibility.

Document outlines

HTML documents can be represented by a document outline that summarizes page structure and improves accessibility.

W3C Warning

Authors are advised to use heading rank (h1-h6) to convey document structure.

WAI-ARIA Roles

ARIA roles help assistive technologies understand page structure and behavior.
